# Retail Sales Performance Analysis Using Python

## 📌 Project Description
This project analyzes the US Superstore sales dataset to uncover trends across 
product categories, regions, customers, and time periods. The goal is to identify 
which categories and regions drive the most revenue, understand seasonal sales 
patterns, and highlight the business's most valuable customers — using Python 
for data cleaning, analysis, and visualization.

## 🔍 Key Insights

**1. Technology drives the most revenue**
Technology is the top-performing category at ~$830,000 in total sales, ahead of 
Furniture (~$730,000) and Office Supplies (~$700,000). At the sub-category level, 
Phones ($327,782) and Chairs ($322,822) are the two highest individual revenue 
generators.

**2. Sales are highly seasonal, peaking at year-end**
Sales peak sharply in November ($268,768) and December ($246,266), likely driven 
by holiday and year-end business purchasing. February is the weakest month 
($131,153) — less than half of November's total.

**3. Revenue is concentrated in a small group of top customers**
The top customer, Sean Miller, alone contributes $25,043 in sales. The top 10 
customers together drive a significant share of total revenue, showing the 
business relies heavily on a small number of high-value customers.

## 💡 Recommendations
- Increase marketing and inventory investment in Technology and Furniture's 
  top sub-categories, since they generate a disproportionate share of revenue.
- Launch Q1 promotional campaigns (especially in February) to offset the 
  seasonal sales dip following the November–December peak.
